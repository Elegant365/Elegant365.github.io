What are OneNote class notebooks?

Class Notebook is a feature of OneNote that allows teachers to incorporate a group of students to work and collaborate together to a greater degree within their classrooms. The OneNote Class Notebook can also be a collaboration space for teachers and students to work together.

As a teacher, you may add students to your class notebook, allowing you to cooperate with them at a greater extent in courses. After the students are added, OneNote automatically allocates 3 sections to each student: 

  • Collaboration Space
  • Content Library
  • Private Notebook


While students cannot access another student’s private notebook and vice versa, teachers will have access to the students' private notebooks. In the collaboration space, teachers and students can work together, allowing you to provide one-to-one feedback to each student. In the content library, teachers can allocate resources to all students, thus saving time during lessons and maximize their teaching time. The private notebook is a personal area for the students to work on their homework or take their own personal notes. You may also provide students with feedback on their homework!

Using the class notebook will allow you as a teacher to maximize your teaching effectivity. It only takes 5 minutes to create a notebook; it is an ideal learning tool to use in the classroom.
