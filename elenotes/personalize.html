How to personalize your notebook?

OneNote allows you to personalize your notes to your own standard. There are features available such as changing the page color, adding rule lines; you can even enable password protection on your notes. 


Let’s say for example that you are revising for your English Literature poetry exam and you need to write annotations for each poem in your anthology. First, you can change the color of the page. To do so, select the view tab, and move your cursor to the section where it mentions “page color”.  In this case, we’ll select a shade of red, as it is associated with blood and violence from war.
 
Allowing your notes to look organized and clean is also one of the key objectives to an excellent notebook when it comes to revising. An appropriate act would be to add rule lines to your annotations. When reading through, your focus will not be lost as easily if you read along with the lines. To achieve this, select “rule lines” in the view tab. You can even set the distances between lines to your preference. By altering the distance between lines, it may allow you to feel more comfortable while reviewing your notes.  

Furthermore, you can secure a password that will prevent others from reviewing or editing your notes.  To enable a password, you will need to move your cursor to the particular section that requires protection. Then right click it. There is a “password protection” sign, and by navigating to this, a password can then be added. To complete this, just enter in your own password and confirm it.
