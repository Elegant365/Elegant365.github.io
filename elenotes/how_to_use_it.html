How to use a OneNote digital notebook?

OneNote is split into many notebooks, which you can use to separate and classify your notes to your specific needs. Each notebook is further split into different sections, you can add more sections or remove existing sections depending on your need. Each section can be further split into many pages. It is on these very pages that you can take your notes. 

On the note-taking pages, there will be 4 tabs on the top menu: Home, Inset, Draw, and View. However, if you have created a separate “Class Notebook”, there will be another tab of the same name. 

The home tab provides you with all the necessary functions that you will need to make notes. It allows you to customize the font, text size, text color, underline words, make them bold, italic, and much more. It also provides a few pre-formatted text options such as “Heading” and “Page Title” which you can incorporate into your note-taking. They format the text in the way shown in the boxes. The home tag also includes tags, which you can use to classify your notes in your own personal way.  
 
On the Insert tab, you can insert shapes, tables, and much more. For example, you can add arrows from the shapes dropdown to visually show steps, and you can insert Math symbols to showcase math equations, etc. This tab also allows you to import files, pictures, and stickers as well. 
 
In the draw tab, the Draw button allows you to draw into your notebook using your trackpad or your mouse. You can change the thickness, color, and pen type of your cursor to further customize your note-taking.  
 
Finally, the View tab is all about the management of your notebook. It allows you to alter the style and color of your notebook and allows you to set passwords for certain sections of your notebook.
